Modern Cordel — The Technology of Now

Adaption of the poem made by Milton Duarte

I'm growing tired of this age Of all this technology We only speak through emails So brief, so cold, no energy Twitter and Facebook reign Before I go insane I'll tell it all poetically
It isn't like the days before Now everything is short and fast “You” is just two letters now The rest was scrapped and didn't last To show someone you care A flower's enough to share A heart emoji's cast
The “at sign” has no weight at all It's just part of an address A dot no longer ends the line It starts a site, no less Now it's always “dot com” If the website is strong Its price will soon impress
And worse, this new language play With symbols made to replace A semicolon, a little star A scribble takes its place One mark means “kiss to you” Another—guess its clue Decipher this coded face
I miss the letters written slow By hand, with heart and care You'd send them in the summertime Arriving late, but rare They carried real emotion Across the miles, devotion A soul revealed right there
But now I write and hit “send” It reaches in a blink of time Yet when I try to talk again The other's left online They've switched to another app The world is caught in trap Of screens that steal our mind
But thankfully, for prayer No internet is required God listens to each voice No password's ever wired Two knees become your colon Your lips the true console And He reads what's inspired
